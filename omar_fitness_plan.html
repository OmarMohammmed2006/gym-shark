Key principles
Progressive overload — the golden rule
Every week, try to add either one more rep or a small amount of extra weight (2.5–5 kg) to each lift. This is how muscles grow. Track your numbers every session — if you're not progressing, you're not growing.
Cardio always goes at the END
Cardio before lifting depletes glycogen and reduces your strength output. Do all your lifting first, then finish with 15–20 min of cardio. This order maximizes both muscle growth and fat burning.
Sleep 7–8 hours — non-negotiable
Muscles grow during recovery, not during the workout. Poor sleep elevates cortisol (a fat-storing, muscle-destroying hormone) and tanks gym performance. No supplement replaces sleep.

Why protein is your #1 priority
At 75 kg, you need ~2g protein per kg = 150g daily. This is the single most important number. Protein preserves and builds muscle while you're in a calorie deficit. Without enough protein you'll lose weight, but it'll be muscle, not fat — leaving you weaker and still soft. Spread your protein across 3–4 meals (35–40g each) for best absorption.

3 simple eating rules
1
Protein first, every meal. Before thinking about anything else on your plate, make sure there's a protein source. Chicken, eggs, tuna, lentils — doesn't matter. Target 35–40g per meal across 4 meals.
2
Time your carbs around workouts. Eat more carbs (rice, oats, bread) in the meal before and after training. Eat fewer on rest days. This maximizes energy during lifts and uses carbs productively.
3
Don't over-restrict. This is a lifestyle change, not a crash diet. Aim for 80–90% clean eating. One bad meal won't ruin your progress — skipping gym days and eating junk consistently will.
Hydration
Drink at least 2.5–3 litres of water per day. More on training days. Dehydration tanks gym performance and is often mistaken for hunger. Plain water, black coffee, and unsweetened tea all count.
